Global Passport Index 2026
The Global Passport Index 2026 evaluates 197 countries and territories across three dimensions, including mobility, investment attractiveness, and quality of life. The findings highlight the countries currently best positioned in an increasingly interconnected world.
The 2026 Index reveals that the gap between the world's strongest and weakest passports continues to widen, reflecting growing disparities in mobility access, economic opportunity, and quality of life.
At the same time, international travel remains shaped by a system that is far less reciprocal than many assume, with fewer than 40 percent of bilateral country relationships operating on an equal visa basis.
While mobility remains a defining measure of passport strength, the strongest passports are distinguished by broader factors, including economic competitiveness, quality of life, and long-term stability.
As a result, passport strength today reflects far more than the number of visa-free destinations.

Sweden
The World's Strongest Passport in 2026
Sweden occupies the highest position in the Global Passport Index 2026.
Its leadership reflects consistent performance across the three dimensions of the index, combining strong international mobility with one of the world's highest standards of living and a highly competitive economic environment.
Unlike countries that excel primarily in mobility, Sweden's position is built on balance. The country performs strongly across quality of life, investment attractiveness, and global access, demonstrating that long-term passport strength depends on a combination of factors rather than mobility alone.
For internationally mobile individuals and families, Sweden represents the benchmark for a well-rounded global passport in 2026.

Case Study · Portugal
From Dubai to Lisbon
“Portugal gave our family something no other programme could — the right to call Europe home, without leaving behind who we are.”
— GCS Client, Dubai · 2024
The Challenge
A family of four from Dubai — dual Indian nationals with strong ties to the UAE — approached GCS seeking a pathway to EU citizenship. Their priorities were clear: broad global access, European healthcare and education for their children, and a country with a strong quality of life.
The Solution
GCS recommended Portugal's Golden Visa programme via an investment fund, aligning with the family's preference for a passive, low-risk route. Residency was granted in 18 months, with Portuguese citizenship approved five years later.
